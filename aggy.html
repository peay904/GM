“The Weight in the Air”

The air is too loud with invisible things,
A rustle of noise that nobody brings.
My skin wears the hours like barbed wire thread,
Thoughts clatter like coins on a floor in my head.

Gravity pulls on the corners of peace,
Stretching the moments where calm should release.
Each breath is a burden, a rusty old hinge—
No door ever opens, it only just cringes.

Nothing is wrong, and yet everything itches,
The silence is shouting in terrible pitches.
A thousand small nothings pile up on my chest,
And none of them tire, and none of them rest.

But—
Even in stormlight, the sky does not break.
Even in fury, the heart learns to ache
In rhythms that soften, like rain on a drum—
The chaos may shout, but the stillness will come.

There’s mercy in minutes, and grace in the grind,
A whisper that kindness is stubborn, not blind.
So let it all buzz, let it clamor and spin—
The world may press out, but I breathe back in.

And here in this breath, this one quiet space,
I find I’m still whole. I’m still here. There is grace.
